My Journey Through CareerFoundry: From Customer Service to UX Design

Posted on June 23, 2024

Introduction

Hi there! I’m Dan Dougherty, and I’m excited to share a bit about my journey with you. I spent years working in customer service, juggling roles as a server, and most recently, general manager. During this time, I discovered my passion for creating seamless and enjoyable experiences for people.

While I loved my time in customer service, I wanted to delve deeper into understanding why certain experiences delighted customers while others left them frustrated. This curiosity led me to UX design. I realized that the skills I had developed—empathy, active listening, and problem-solving—were perfectly aligned with UX design principles.

I chose CareerFoundry because of their hands-on approach, real-world projects, and supportive community. Their program promised not just education but transformation. I eagerly dove into learning user research, wireframing, prototyping, and creating designs that resonate with users.

Now, having completed the course, I’m excited to share my journey with you. From late-night study sessions to “aha” moments that made everything click, it’s been an incredible ride. So, grab a cup of coffee (or your favorite beverage), and let’s dive into the world of UX design through my eyes.

Discovering UX Design

My journey into UX design began almost by accident, but in hindsight, it feels like it was always meant to be. As a creative-minded person with a love for technology, I had been exploring various aspects of design and development for years. It all started with teaching myself Photoshop and Illustrator, diving into graphic design. This creative exploration eventually led me to UI design through courses on Coursera and Google's offerings.

While UI design fascinated me, I soon realized that I wanted to understand more about what makes a website function seamlessly, not just look good. This curiosity led me to frontend development. I even attended a summer bootcamp hosted by a local design company, BizStream, where I delved deeper into coding and creating interactive websites. It was an amazing experience that I participated in for two summers, further solidifying my love for creating and building.

However, there was still something missing. Coding alone didn't satisfy my desire to understand the complete user experience. During the COVID-19 pandemic, I revisited my interest in UI design but quickly realized that I needed something more holistic. After the pandemic, my girlfriend and I moved to Tennessee, where I started working as a general manager at a local tourist restaurant/retail shop. This job brought a new level of satisfaction as I began designing merchandise and apparel for car enthusiasts who visited from all over the country to drive the infamous "Tail of the Dragon."

Designing for the shop was a thrilling experience. Seeing people enjoy and purchase my creations was immensely rewarding. I started showing my designs to regular customers, gathering their feedback, and making iterative changes based on their input. It was during this process that everything clicked. I realized that I loved the research and development stages of design, the feedback loop, and making sure my creations perfectly met users' needs and preferences.

This epiphany led me to discover UX design. It was the perfect blend of creativity, technology, and user-centered thinking that I had been searching for. After researching various UX design courses and bootcamps, I chose CareerFoundry. Their comprehensive curriculum, supportive community, and focus on practical, real-world skills made it the ideal choice for me. Now, I am on a journey to become a skilled UX designer, ready to create meaningful and engaging user experiences.

However, the transition wasn't without its challenges. Initially, I had some misconceptions about UX design. I thought it was primarily about creating visually appealing interfaces. I quickly learned that while aesthetics are important, UX design is fundamentally about solving problems and enhancing user experiences. Understanding the importance of user research and usability testing was a significant shift in my perspective. There was also a learning curve in mastering various tools and methodologies. But with determination and the support of the CareerFoundry community and mentors, I overcame these challenges and grew more confident in my skills.

CareerFoundry Experience

Enrolling in the CareerFoundry UX Design course was a transformative journey that solidified my passion for user-centered design. The course structure was thoughtfully designed, combining theoretical modules, practical assignments, and real-world projects. Each module built upon the previous one, ensuring a comprehensive understanding of UX principles.

One of the most impactful modules was user research. Here, I learned the intricacies of gathering and analyzing user data to inform design decisions. Conducting user interviews, creating personas, and developing user flows were eye-opening experiences that highlighted the importance of empathy in design. Another significant module focused on wireframing and prototyping. This module allowed me to translate user needs into tangible designs, iterating on them based on feedback and usability testing.

The highlight of the course was undoubtedly the PlutoPay project. This project involved designing a seamless online payment experience, from initial user research to high-fidelity prototyping. I spent countless hours conducting user interviews, analyzing pain points, and iterating on designs. The hands-on nature of the project provided invaluable insights into the practical application of UX principles.

A memorable aspect of the CareerFoundry course was the constant feedback and support from mentors and peers. Weekly check-ins, detailed critiques, and collaborative discussions helped me refine my skills and stay motivated. One key takeaway from this experience is the importance of iterative testing and incorporating user feedback at every stage of the design process. Additionally, the course emphasized the need for empathy, teaching me to always consider the user's perspective in my designs.

Overall, the CareerFoundry UX Design course not only equipped me with essential skills but also instilled a deep appreciation for the user-centered approach. The knowledge and experiences gained have prepared me to tackle real-world design challenges with confidence and creativity.

Skills Developed

Throughout my journey in the CareerFoundry UX Design course, I developed a suite of core skills essential to creating user-centered designs.

  • User Research: I learned to conduct comprehensive user research, including interviews, surveys, and contextual inquiries. These techniques allowed me to gather valuable insights into user needs, behaviors, and pain points, which informed my design decisions.
  • Usability Testing: Usability testing became a cornerstone of my design process. I conducted tests to identify usability issues and iterated on my designs based on user feedback. This skill ensures that my designs are intuitive and user-friendly.
  • Wireframing: I mastered the art of creating wireframes to visualize the structure and layout of my designs. Wireframing helped me quickly iterate on ideas and communicate design concepts effectively.
  • Prototyping: Building on wireframing, I learned to create high-fidelity prototypes using tools like Adobe XD and Figma. Prototyping allowed me to test interactions and gather feedback before moving to the development stage.
  • Interaction Design: Understanding how users interact with interfaces is crucial. I honed my skills in designing intuitive and engaging interactions that enhance the user experience.
  • Heuristic Evaluation: I became adept at conducting heuristic evaluations to identify usability issues based on established principles. This skill ensures that my designs adhere to best practices and are user-friendly.
  • Creating User Personas: I learned to develop detailed user personas based on research data. Personas help me keep the user's needs and goals at the forefront of my design process.
  • Developing User Flows: Mapping out user flows allowed me to visualize the user's journey through a product. This skill helps in identifying potential friction points and ensuring a seamless user experience.

My background in customer service played a significant role in my understanding of user needs and improving user experience. In my previous roles, I developed strong interpersonal skills and learned the importance of empathy and active listening. These experiences have given me a unique perspective in UX design, where understanding and addressing user needs is paramount. My ability to communicate effectively and gather feedback has been invaluable in conducting user research and usability testing. The problem-solving skills honed in customer service have also translated well into identifying and addressing usability issues in my designs. Overall, my customer service background has enriched my approach to UX design, making me more attuned to creating solutions that truly resonate with users.

Projects and Case Studies

During my time in the CareerFoundry UX Design course, I worked on several impactful projects. Here, I’ll provide an overview of two significant ones: the PlutoPay project and my Personal Portfolio Website.

PlutoPay: Streamlining Online Financial Transactions

Problem: The goal of the PlutoPay project was to create a responsive web app that allows users to shop, transfer money, and manage finances without the need for physical debit or credit cards. The challenge was to design a secure, efficient, and user-friendly solution for online transactions.

Approach: I began with comprehensive user research, including interviews and surveys, to understand user pain points and needs. I developed user personas and mapped out user flows to identify critical friction points. With this foundation, I created wireframes and interactive prototypes using Adobe XD, iterating on the design based on user feedback.

Solution: The final design was a streamlined, intuitive payment interface that minimized user effort and enhanced the overall experience. Key features included simplified navigation, a secure authentication process, clear calls to action, and real-time feedback during the transaction process. The app also incorporated additional features like budgeting and goal setting.

Results: Usability testing showed a significant improvement in user satisfaction and a reduction in drop-off rates during the payment process. The design was well-received by users, who found it much easier to navigate and complete transactions.

Personal Portfolio Website

Problem: As part of my journey to break into the UX design field, I needed a modern, responsive portfolio website to showcase my skills and projects. The challenge was to create a site that not only highlighted my work but also demonstrated my design and development capabilities.

Approach: I conducted a competitive analysis to gather insights on effective portfolio designs. I then created wireframes and prototypes, focusing on a clean, user-friendly layout. The development process involved HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, ensuring the site was fully responsive and optimized for various devices.

Solution: The final product is a sleek, modern portfolio website that showcases my UX design projects and skills. It includes detailed case studies, interactive elements, and a blog section to share my thoughts on design trends and experiences.

Results: The portfolio has been a valuable tool in my job search, receiving positive feedback from peers and mentors. It effectively communicates my skills and experiences, helping potential employers understand my design approach and capabilities.

Overcoming Challenges

Every journey has its hurdles, and my path through the CareerFoundry UX Design course was no exception. Here are some of the key challenges I faced and how I overcame them:

  • Balancing Work and Study: Juggling a full-time job while committing to an intensive UX design course was a significant challenge. I learned to manage my time effectively, setting aside dedicated hours each day for study and project work. This experience taught me the importance of discipline and time management.
  • Mastering New Tools and Techniques: Transitioning from a background in customer service to UX design required learning new tools like Adobe XD, and various prototyping and testing software. Initially, this steep learning curve was overwhelming. However, through consistent practice, online tutorials, and feedback from mentors and peers, I gradually became proficient in these tools.
  • Conducting User Research: Conducting user research remotely posed its own set of challenges. Reaching out to users, scheduling interviews, and gathering meaningful insights were not always straightforward. I overcame this by leveraging online survey tools, video conferencing, and maintaining clear and consistent communication with participants.
  • Iterating on Designs Based on Feedback: Receiving critical feedback and iterating on designs can be tough, especially when you've put a lot of effort into your initial concepts. I learned to view feedback as an opportunity for improvement rather than criticism. This mindset shift allowed me to embrace iterative design, continuously refining my work based on user and mentor feedback.
  • Collaborating Remotely: With the course being fully remote, collaboration with peers and mentors was sometimes challenging due to differing time zones and communication barriers. I improved my collaboration skills by using project management tools, maintaining regular check-ins, and being proactive in seeking and giving feedback.

These challenges taught me invaluable lessons in persistence, problem-solving, and adaptability. I learned that setbacks are not roadblocks but opportunities to grow and improve. This mindset has not only strengthened my skills as a UX designer but also prepared me to tackle future challenges with confidence and resilience.
